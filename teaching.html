Intermediate Econometrics: Microeconometrics

Teaching Assistant · Department of Economics, PUCP · 2026

I design and teach practical sessions on empirical methods for causal inference and applied microeconometrics.

Topics include randomized controlled trials, instrumental variables, difference-in-differences, regression discontinuity, matching methods, panel data, censored and truncated outcomes, and machine learning.

Lecture slides Problem sets Code examples Videos

Teaching approach

My teaching emphasizes the connection between econometric assumptions, identification strategies, computational implementation, and the interpretation of empirical results.

I use examples in Stata, Python, and R to illustrate how methods are implemented in applied research.
